Save 10 minutes a day with these efficient kitchen tools

Cooking your own food is time-consuming, but incredibly satisfying and healthy. Many of us eat out or have ready to cook stuff in the fridge, just because our busy lifestyles give us little time to spend making your own food.

Like all boring tasks, you can make your daily cooking time enjoyable with a few efficient kitchen tools that saves you a lot of time.

Efficient Kitchen Tools

  1. Peeler

Can you believe that I didn’t have a peeler in my kitchen for almost 2 years after I started cooking along? I admit that I’m not the most frequent cook, but I peeled all of those 13 apples and 4 zucchini’s with a knife. When I discovered the peeler, it just changed my life. I’m pretty sure that you have one at home, but if not, get  your peeler here.

2.  Hand Blender

Machines were invented to make our lives easier. The invention of the electric motor and the reduction in size made many things possible. The  hand blender is one of the most useful multi-purpose kitchen tools – Juicing, baby food, cashew nut pastes and hundreds of uses yet to be discovered!

The best part?

It’s super easy to clean.

 

3.  Kitchen Timer

Are you a multi-tasker? Do you often find burnt food or milt that boils over? You’ve been missing  kitchen timer. The classy natural cook you are deserves nothing less than a  mechanical stainless steel timer ($11) that not only keeps you alert, but incredibly satisfying to wind.

 

 

4.  Good Knives

If you think you’ll spend time the kitchen cooking real food, it definitely involves  a lot of slicing and dicing. You will not believe the difference a  good set of knives makes to small tasks such as slicing onions or tomatoes. A good set of knives are one of the most efficient kitchen tool that you’ll  never regret investing in.

5.  Dish Dryer

If you don’t have a dishwasher, one of the most time consuming tasks in the kitchen is cleaning your dishes and drying them. Having a dish dryer that let’s you easily organise your dishes after you wash save you more time than you would imagine. Make sure you get  a stainless steel one and not a cheap plastic one.

6. Bluetooth Headphones

If you’re worried about “wasting” time in the kitchen that you could be spending else where like personal development, podcasts are a great idea. A lightweight pair of bluetooth headphones helps you double your efficiency in the kitchen. You can also use your kitchen time to talk to friends and family, listen to cooking instructions from your mom or even chill out with your favourite music. A pair of  decent headphones that cost around $10 would be more than enough if you’re not much of an audiophile.

7. Kitchen Sink Clog Remover

People often underestimate the time spent on cleaning up after your cook. If cook regularly, you must be familiar with clogged kitchen sinks. This little friend makes it easy to remove clogs that saves you a few minutes each time this happens and makes sure you don’t end up messy.  Get one of these for just $14 here.
