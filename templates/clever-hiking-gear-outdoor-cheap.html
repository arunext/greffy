5 inexpensive hiking gear that are outright clever

Summer is almost here – at least in the northern hemisphere. The adventurers amongst us are already planning summer trips and dreaming of the wild. If you’ve been a regular hiker, you would know the difference that good hiking gear makes to your adventures.

7 clever hiking gear in 2018

Mosquito Net face protector

Depending on where you’re going, mosquitoes could be a big menace. While you can cover most of your body with clothes, your face remains vulnerable. A  mesh mosquito net ($1.65) can protect you from mosquitos and bees, is easily foldable to carry along and lets plenty of air in.


Solar Mobile Charger

As you might know, finding electricity while you’re outdoors is close to impossible. But hey, there’s plenty of sun everywhere. A  solar mobile charger ($9) if your go to hiking gear to ensure peace of mind while you wander around in the wild.

Cooking Tripod

When early men discovered fire and started cooking, they wished they had something to hang the food from. They would go crazy if you showed them one of these  cooking tripods ($18) to hang food from while they burnt wood they collect below. And yeah, ideally they would want one of these  5L cooking potss ($15) too.

Drinking water filter

Usually on most good hikes, you end up passing through water bodies. Although the water is usually potable, it’s still better to filter it. Why risk screwing up your entire trip just because you drank contaminated water? And it is totally avoidable with a  handy water filter ($5).

Hiking backpack

If you don’t have one yet make sure you get a good quality  waterproof climbing backpack ($20)  to carry all your cool gadgets, clothes and food.

Happy Hiking!
