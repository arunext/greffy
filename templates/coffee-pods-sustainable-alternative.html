5 environment friendly alternatives to coffee pods

I’m a coffee person.

I also care about the environment.

When I saw that coffee pods take up to 500 years to breakdown on my facebook feed, I went searching (online) for sustainable alternatives that are equally convenient.

Finding convenient alternatives to everyday tasks is very much part of evolution. As we keep breaking our productivity records, the need for coffee in our daily routine keeps increasing. This takes a toll on the environment which even  Nespresso’s ex-boss agrees.

Let’s take a look and see how we can play our small part in saving the world.

Sustainable alternatives to coffee pods

Italian coffee maker or the Moka Pot

The  Italian coffee maker brews coffee by passing boiling water pressurised by steam through the coffee powder in the middle compartment. The most common models are stove-top but electric ones are not uncommon. It takes me about a minute to make black coffee. I usually microwave the milk at the same time to save time and mix them when they’re both done. If you have a large can of coffee powder, the per-cup plastic produced is nearly zero. You can  buy one for as low as $11.

Stainless Steel Pods

These are probably my favourite of the lot. They’re sustainable, doesn’t compromise on convenience and are inexpensive in the short run. Buy a  bunch of them ($8/piece), fill them with your favourite coffee powder and use them like regular coffee pods. Rinse. Repeat.

Compostable pods

Almost everything is great about coffee pods. The convenience. The efficiency of brewing a single cup. Everything, except for the environmental impact. And that’s why many companies such as Keurig has come up with partially bio-degradable pods. Some companies such as Wolfgang Puck and  Organic Coffee Company have gone all the way to make coffee pods that can be disposed off along with food waste making is a great alternative to traditional coffee pods.

Caffeine pills

If all you care is the alertness and energy that you feel after coffee and not the taste, caffeine pills are a great choice. And we are very sure that they are biodegradable since our bodies can break them down easily. They have also been linked to high performance and weight loss. However, there is risk of overdose which could cause irritability, dizziness, rapid heartbeat, and mental fogginess.

Automatic Brewing machine

If you really care about nature and are ready to shell out $500, super automatic espresso machines are the way to go. Many of them are simple to use, easy to maintain and of course sustainable. Press a button and you have your coffee. Check out the following video to see if these ones are for you.

All said, I think coffee pods are here to stay. I can imagine the future generations who grow up on coffee pods come back from vacation and say, “My papa still uses a Moka pot which makes the best coffee ever. I wonder where he gets the patience.”
